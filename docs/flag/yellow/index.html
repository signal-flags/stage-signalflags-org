Yellow flag

Racing Rules of Sailing

A yellow flag is used when taking a scoring penalty under RRS 44.3. It is also used in the start sequence for windsurfing fleet races under RRS Appendix B B3 and for a number of purposes in RRS Appendix C: Match Racing.

Finally, the special procedures for Rule 42 in RRS Appendix P use a yellow flag in P1.2 to indicate a penalty.

Beach safety

In a beach safety context a yellow flag usually indicates 'medium hazard'. The International Life Saving Federation gives the following description:

Moderate surf and/or currents are present. Weak swimmers are discouraged from entering the water. For others, enhanced care and caution should be exercised.

ICS flag O - Oscar

The Internataional Code of Signals flag Oscar is also a plain yellow flag: the images used on this site are identical.
